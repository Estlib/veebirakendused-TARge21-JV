Iterative model

Pros and Cons

PROS CONS
Potential defects are spotted and dealt with early More resources may be required
Functional prototypes are developed early in the project life cycle Each successive phase is rigid with no overlaps
Less time is spent on documenting and more on designing The need for more intensive project management may be required
Progress is easily measured Issues about system architecture may turn out to be a constraining factor because of the lack of a full requirements specification for the entire system
Changes to project scope are less costly and easier to implement It may be difficult to pin down an end date for the project conclusion
Testing is facilitated by the modules being relatively small Highly skilled talent is required for risk analysis
Most risks can be identified during iteration and higher risks can be dealt with as an early priority
Successive iterations can be managed easily as milestones
An operational product is delivered with every iteration
Operating time is reduced
Customer feedback is based on working products rather than technical specifications
